# Burners Invocations

*There are things in the Dragon Isles older than the kingdoms and hungrier than the sea. They do not answer prayers. They answer **names** — spoken thrice, aloud, by someone reckless enough to want something badly. An Invocation is not a spell. It is a bargain, and the other party has been making bargains since before your language had words.*

An **Invocation** is how a Burner calls on an otherworldly being — a god, a dead power, a d'jinn, a thing from beyond the veil — by its **True Name**, and asks. If it answers, it makes an **Offer** and an **Ask**. What you can get is capped by your **Alignment with that patron**; what it costs is always real.

Invocations are **not Sorcerie**. No Arcana, no spellbook, no school Trait. Any Burner may attempt one — the fighter kneeling in the barrow-dark has exactly the same standing as the archmage, and often a better one, if her Traits lean the patron's way.

---

## Patrons

A **patron** is any otherworldly being with a True Name and a **nature** — the shape of what it loves, hates, and wants. The gods of the Isles qualify: every one answers to one of the **seven divine archetypes** — Sun King, Earth Mother, Winged Messenger, Veiled Maiden, Winter King, Voiceless Keeper, and the Voice in the Void — and each culture knows them by its own names (Pyrose the Fyrelord, Xohm the Earthmother, Mrmym the Sky Dragon, Lunara the Maiden of Song, Hrivlygg the Frost King, Hruul the Voiceless One, and stranger voices besides — see *Gods of the Dragon Isles* in the world canon). So do lesser and stranger things: a drowned king remembered by one village, a d'jinn in a brass lamp, a deep one under the harbor, the nameless thing a cult calls *the Gardener*.

**Nature is the rulebook.** Every patron has a nature the Guide holds — what requests it likes, what it refuses, what it shapes its callers toward. The gods seldom leave their Hallows; they lend power through those aligned to them, and the stranger things deal more directly and less kindly.

**The Guide's patron card.** For each patron in play, the Guide keeps a short private line: its **nature** (two or three words), what it **favors**, what it **refuses**, and a **shaping table** of d6 Traits it molds its callers toward. A patron of storms might shape *wrathful, restless, sea-longing, loud, fearless, scarred by lightning*; a patron of the veiled moon might shape *patient, secret-keeping, night-eyed, quiet, dream-touched, faithful*.

## Alignment with a Patron

Your standing with a patron is an **Alignment** (see *Alignment* in [[Burners Adventure Game]]): count every Trait across all your Approaches that bears on the patron's nature, **divide by 4, round down**. Record it when it matters: *Lunara 2*, *the Drowned King 1*.

It grows the way everything in Burners grows — through play:

- **Traits.** Take Traits at level-up that lean the patron's way (their shaping Traits count double-sure).
- **Deeds.** Acts in the patron's nature are Traits waiting to be named — the Guide may point at one you've earned.
- **Relics.** Carrying the patron's **Relic** counts **+1 Alignment** with them, for Invocations only.
- **Shaping.** Accepting a pact's shaping Trait (below) deepens the bond by definition.

| Alignment | Standing | At the table |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Stranger | The patron may not hear; an Offer, if any, is thin |
| **1** | Known | Small favors, one clear Ask |
| **2** | Favored | Serious Offers; shaping sticks |
| **3+** | Saint | Their business is yours — see *Saints* |

Patron Alignment is not Sorcerie. A Sorcerer may be a stranger to every god; a torch-hand may be Lunara 3 and never cast a spell in her life.

## True Names

You cannot invoke what you cannot name. **True Names** hide in myth — runestones, tomb-glyphs, amulet inscriptions, grimoire margins, the waking name of a Relic, the last word of a dying cultist. Beings have **many names**; part of the lore game is learning which ones are true.

Finding or verifying a name is play: a **`2d6 + Sorcerie`** roll (ancient lore) or **`2d6 + Craft`** (decipherment, legwork) against Heat, or simply the reward of exploration. *Have I heard this name before? Is this the same being the fisherfolk call the Weeping Mother?* — good questions for the Guide, and every answer is Lore worth a margin note.

## Making an Invocation

1. **Declare.** Speak the True Name **thrice**, aloud and formal, and state your request in the fiction — what you want, not a spell title. In combat this is an **Action**; out of combat it is a beat the scene waits on.
2. **Pay to be heard.** The call costs **1 Heat** for the crew, or **1 HP** from the caller — nerve, not meat. Paid whether or not anyone answers.
3. **Roll.** **`2d6 + Alignment with the patron`** against the Guide's **Cost** — often the scene's Heat, or **8 + the scale of the ask**: 8 for a small favor, 10 for a serious working, 12 for a world-tilting demand. A request **against the patron's nature** raises the Cost by 2. A request squarely **in their nature**, backed by deeds already done, may drop it by 2 — or simply succeed (Step 1, as any well-backed deed can).
4. **Answer.** Meet the Cost and the patron **responds**. Fall short: silence, a wrong listener, or a price paid for nothing — the Heat or HP is not refunded, and in a bad place the wrong thing may have heard its name spoken thrice.

*The workings of otherworldly beings are mysterious. They may read your request from a different angle and a different sense of time. Word requests with care, or don't — the stories are better when you don't.*

## The Offer and the Ask

When a patron responds, the Guide sets an **Offer level** from **1** up to your **Alignment with the patron**. The Offer is what the being will do; the Ask is what it wants back.

**Offers by level** (guidance, not a menu):

| Level | The patron will… |
|---|---|
| **1** | A nudge — a door unbarred, a scent on the wind, a candle that will not gutter, a name whispered back |
| **2** | A hand — turn the shambling dead from your path, calm a storm long enough to land, mark the liar in the room, close a wound that will not stanch |
| **3** | An arm — rebuke a willful dead thing in its name, part the flood, strike one foe with its wrath, carry a message across the world |
| **4+** | A miracle — and no patron grants one lightly, nor cheaply |

**Asks by nature** (d6, or the Guide picks):

| d6 | The Ask |
|---|---|
| 1 | A **quest** — do a deed in its nature, on a clock |
| 2 | A **taboo** — never again do the thing it hates |
| 3 | A **shaping Trait** — become more like its saint (below) |
| 4 | **Heat owed** — the crew carries +2 Heat until a deed discharges it |
| 5 | A **Relic errand** — find, carry, or return something of its |
| 6 | A **tithe** — treasure, blood, a secret, a song; paid now |

You may **accept or refuse**. Refusing costs nothing beyond what you paid to be heard. Accept, and the patron delivers; the Ask is yours until cleared — and patrons remember defaulters better than debtors.

## Shaping

Patrons mold their callers toward their ideal **saint**. On an accepted pact, the Guide rolls (or picks) from the patron's **shaping table** and names a Trait — *sea-longing*, *wrathful*, *grave-keeper*, *patient as stone*.

- **Already have it?** You are in harmony — no cost, and the patron is pleased.
- **Don't?** Take it at your **next level-up**, free, like any Trait — or, where the pact is narrow, the Guide may offer it as a **Signature** on a Trait you already hold.
- **Refuse the shaping?** The pact may sour, or the Ask worsens. Patrons do not forget being told no.

Shaping is how Alignment compounds: each pact leaves a Trait that bears on the patron, and the count climbs.

## Relics

A **Relic** is a sacred object that serves as **conduit and ark** to a patron — a rallying flag for its power. To the uninitiated a Relic may pass for a cursed or sentient item, since the patron can act through it.

- **Ark.** Carrying a patron's Relic counts **+1 Alignment** with that patron, for Invocations only.
- **Conduit.** A Relic is often a **legendary item** or **legendary Arcana** — its true name may be the patron's gift, surrendered through an Invocation, and the name wakes the third die (see *Legendary Items* in [[Burners Adventure Game]]).
- **Ledger.** A Relic in the wrong hands is a grievance; returning one is the classic Ask.

**Sentient items** are the speaking subset — a bound soul or a splinter of the patron itself, capricious, jealous, and unhinged from confinement. A sentient Relic may aid, argue, or betray; it hates being abandoned, rejected, or "cheated on" with a rival item. Carrying one grows your Alignment whether you like where it's growing or not.

---

## Saints

At **Alignment 3+** with a patron, holding their Relic, you may stand as their **saint** for a quest the patron cares about.

- **Blessing.** You may bless companions who take up the quest — those who accept pursue it with fervor, and the Guide may grant the blessing teeth (a bonus die at a decisive moment, a Morale that holds, a wound that waits). Fiction first; no new subsystem.
- **The price.** A blessed quest abandoned — even by death — leaves the patron a claim. It may **shape** the defaulter's spirit, or collect in stranger coin. Saints are fearless for a reason: their souls are already spoken for.

## The Willful Dead

The mindless dead burn (see *We Burn Undead* in [[Burners Adventure Game]]). The **willful** dead — the lich, the wraith-lord, the vampire in his hall — are an Invocation problem. A Burner aligned to a being whose nature opposes the grave may **rebuke them in the patron's name**: an Invocation, rolled on Alignment, its Offer the rout or barring of the dead thing. That is the Isles' true "turning" — not your holiness, but the patron's, lent through the pact, at the price all pacts carry.

## Worked Example

*Sefa (Craft 1, Traits: marsh-lore, night-fisher, drowned-kin, quiet) stands in the flooded crypt as the Cellarer's wight rises. She knows the name of **Maren-under-the-Waves**, the drowned mother the fen-folk still sing to — and her Traits lean that way: drowned-kin, night-fisher, marsh-lore bear on the patron. Three Traits ÷ 4 is 0 — but she carries Maren's barnacled comb, a Relic looted two delves back: **+1, Alignment 1.***

*She speaks the name thrice — 1 HP, her voice shaking — and asks Maren to take this dead thing home. The request is squarely in the patron's nature (the drowned belong to her), so the Guide sets Cost 8. Sefa rolls 2d6+1: **9.** The sea answers.*

*Offer, level 1: the crypt's floodwater rises black and cold, and the wight wades down into it as into a mother's arms — gone, not destroyed. The Ask: return the comb to the fen shrine before the next full moon, and Maren names a shaping Trait: **sea-longing**. Sefa accepts. Next level-up, the Trait is hers — and her Alignment with Maren starts to climb.*

## Quick Reference

```
INVOCATION
WHO     any Burner who knows the True Name
ALIGN   Traits bearing on the patron ÷ 4 (round down); +1 with their Relic
CALL    Name thrice + request; 1 Heat (crew) or 1 HP (caller); Action in combat
ROLL    2d6 + Alignment vs Cost (scene Heat, or 8/10/12 by scale;
        ±2 by the patron's nature)
OFFER   level 1 … Alignment (nudge / hand / arm / miracle)
ASK     quest · taboo · shaping Trait · Heat owed · Relic errand · tithe
SHAPE   patron Trait at next level-up (free) or a Signature; refusal sours
NOT     spells · Arcana · Sorcerie — any Burner may call
```
