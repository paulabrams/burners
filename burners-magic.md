# Burners Magic

*How a Sorcerer prepares, carries, and spends magic.*

Magic is prepared, not spontaneous — but the preparation lives in **you**, not in a wand
someone can snatch. A Sorcerer exists in two realms at once, and their pack proves it.

## Spells Are Cards

Think of every spell you know as a card.

- Your **spellbook** is your **deck** — the full library of what you know.
- Your **hand** is your prepared loadout — the spells ready to cast right now.
- Your **Arcana** sets your **hand size**.
- **Casting** a spell **taps** its card.
- **Burning** a card exiles it to pay for more casting.
- A night's **sleep (8 hours)** untaps every tapped card and returns every burned one.

Every card in your hand is in exactly one zone:

| Zone        | Meaning                                                              | Comes back                    |
| ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **Ready**   | In hand, untapped — cast it any time.                                | —                             |
| **Tapped**  | Cast. In your discard, or *in play* while a lasting effect runs.     | Sleep — or a burn recasts it. |
| **Burned**  | Exiled as fuel. Gone.                                                | Sleep. Nothing else.          |

Tapped and burned are very different fates. A tapped card can be bought back with a burn.
A burned card is spent until you rest — you cannot burn a card to recover a burned card,
and no working, no gold, no clever reading brings one back early.

## The Sorcerer Gate

To work magic you must be a **Sorcerer — Sorcerie 1 or higher.** Below that you are a
layman, and the cards mean nothing in your hands.

**One gate stands on high magic: your Sorcerie is your caster level.** You may know, hold,
and cast spells of level up to your Sorcerie. A Sorcerie-1 mage works only 1st-level
spells; a Sorcerie-6 up to 6th. There is no vessel gate anymore — no relic to find that
lets a first-rank sorcerer throw a 6th. The power is in the practitioner.

**A school is trained-only.** Each of the seven people-schools keeps its own secret
language and spell list; you must hold a Trait in that school to touch it at all. The
common Sorcerie school asks no Trait — being a Sorcerer is grammar enough.

**Canting is the exception.** The rogue's school rolls Craft, and a Journeyman (Craft 1+)
may learn, hold, and cast its spells. See [Canting: Kicks and Jarks](#canting-kicks-and-jarks).

## Arcana Slots

You have twelve item slots. As a Sorcerer, you **declare a split**: so many slots are
**physical**, so many are **Arcana**. The Arcana slots are your reach into the magical
realm — they are slots, not items. Your count of Arcana slots is your **hand size**: each
Arcana slot holds **one card** of your hand.

- **Re-split on rest.** On any 8-hour sleep you may redeclare the split. A Sorcerer who
  wants a six-card hand walks into the dark with six slots of gear; power is paid for in
  rope, rations, and armor left behind.
- **Sorcerie caps the card.** Any Arcana slot may hold a spell of any level up to your
  Sorcerie. Slots are not leveled.
- **Your hand is personal.** The spells live in you, not in your gear. No thief, no
  disarm, no dropped pack parts a Sorcerer from their hand. Only sleep, burning, and
  your own choices move the cards.

### Wizard Drip

An Arcana slot may *additionally* carry a physical item — but only **Wizard Drip**: a
staff, wand, rod, orb, ring, runed robe, talisman, the costume of the craft. Some call
these items Arcana when they ride in an Arcana slot; strictly, the slot is the Arcana and
the staff is just a very good-looking stick.

- Drip items keep their mundane function. A wizard's staff in an Arcana slot is still a
  staff — swing it, lean on it, bar a door with it.
- **Never** armor, weapons (beyond the staff's honest whack), shields, or plain cargo. An
  Arcana slot cannot carry fifty feet of rope. That restriction is the price of the split.
- Losing drip loses an item, nothing more. Steal the archmage's staff and you have stolen
  a stick. The magic never lived there.

## Casting

**No spontaneous magic.** You cannot cast a card that is not ready in your hand.

**The cast.**

1. **Say what you do** — name the spell and its effect in the fiction.
2. **Pay the Cost.** In combat, casting is one Action: commit at least L Fuel dice
   (L = the working's level) and roll them for the **cast total**, as ever. Out of combat,
   roll **2d6 + Sorcerie** against the Referee's Cost. (The dice economy is unchanged —
   Sparks, Double Spicy fumbles, and Defending against a spell all run as written in the
   core rules.)
3. **Tap the card.** An instant working goes to your discard as it resolves. A lasting
   working stays **in play** for its duration, then joins the discard.

## Burning

When it comes down to the wire: what will you burn? What won't you need again before you
rest?

You may **burn** cards from your hand as part of a cast Action — no extra time, no extra
roll — to do one of two things:

- **Recast** a spell in your discard, or
- **Copy** a spell you have in play — a second working alongside the first. Place the
  burned card face-down beneath the active one to mark the copy.

**The rules of the fire:**

- **Hand only.** Fuel comes from ready, uncast cards. You cannot burn from your discard,
  from play, or from the burned pile. Spent potential is not fuel; only live potential is.
- **Levels must cover the spell.** The burned cards' levels must sum to **at least** the
  level of the spell being recast or copied. Several small cards may combine; any overage
  is wasted. Two 1sts recast a 2nd. A 4th burned for a 3rd is a 4th burned.
- **Scrolls never burn.** See [Scrolls](#scrolls).
- **Burned is burned.** The cards return at your next 8-hour sleep and no sooner.

Every card in your hand is a cast — and every card is also fuel for a repeat of something
better. You cannot have both. Burning *Knock* to throw a second *Dragon's Breath* is
deciding, here and now, that there are no more locked doors between you and the way out.

## Changing Your Hand

Choosing or changing the cards in your hand requires your **spellbook** (or another
readable source you carry that holds the spell — see *Spellbooks* below).

- **Rest: full redraw.** On an 8-hour sleep with a readable source in reach, rebuild your
  hand freely from your deck — any known spells, up to your Sorcerie, one per Arcana slot.
- **One hour: swap one card.** With an hour of study and the source in hand, exchange one
  card in your hand for one from your deck. **The incoming card inherits the outgoing
  card's status** — swap out a tapped card and the new card arrives tapped. The swap
  changes *what* you can cast, never *how much*.

## Scrolls

A scroll is a spell already written and primed — a physical item that rides in an
**Arcana slot** and **is that slot's card** until cast.

- **Single use.** Cast it and it is spent — ash, blank vellum. The slot it occupied counts
  as holding a tapped card until you sleep and redraw.
- **Cast only.** A scroll can never be burned as fuel. Its magic is already committed to
  one shape; there is nothing loose in it to spend.
- No spellbook is needed to slot a scroll — it arrives pre-loaded. Anyone who can use the
  school's magic may cast it (Sorcerer; Journeyman for Canting).
- Scribing costs and *Read Magic* identification are unchanged: **100 gp × the spell's
  level** in reagents, downtime work, from any spell you hold in a readable source.

## Ritual Casting

A known spell can still be worked straight from the open book — no card, no slot — but
slowly, rooted in place, and only while you sustain it. Ten minutes per spell level;
leave the circle or close the book and the working collapses retroactively. What a
ritual puts in your *mind* stays. Ritual casting taps nothing and burns nothing; the cost
is time and stillness. Never a combat option.

## Spellbooks

Your deck is a book, and a book can be lost.

- **Personal spellbook** — 1 physical slot. Holds every spell you have scribed into it.
  Left in camp, your deck is out of reach: no redraws, no swaps until you are reunited.
- **Readable sources** — a captured grimoire you can read (via school literacy or *Read
  Magic*) serves as deck pages while you carry it.
- **Moving, copying, and duplicating** spells between books is unchanged: reagent inks at
  **100 gp × level** to move a spell in (destroying the source page), **25 gp × level** to
  copy within your own library. Wise sorcerers keep a duplicate in the vault.

## Canting: Kicks and Jarks

Rogues split their twelve slots the same way — but a rogue would never say *Arcana*.

- An Arcana slot is a **kick** — a secret pocket sewn where honest folk carry rope. *"How
  many kicks you running tonight?"*
- The spellbook is the **reader** — the pocketbook of the trade, written in flash paper
  and brotherhood marks.
- A scroll is a **jark** — a counterfeit seal, primed and single-use, spent when flashed.

All the card rules apply: Craft is the rogue's caster level in place of Sorcerie, hand
size is kick count, tapping, burning, and sleep run identically.

**Canting drip is different in the fiction — a lot more black.** Where the Sorcerer's
slots demand the costume of the tower, the rogue's kicks hold the working kit: rope,
picks, a dark lantern, a betty, chalk, a muffler, soft-soled stampers — anything
**concealable** under a coat. Nothing that shines, nothing that rattles.

- **No weapons, armor, or shields** in a kick — with the burglar's exception: a **knife**,
  a **sap**, or a **sling** may hide there.
- A searched rogue looks like a burglar, not a mage. That is the point.

## What Changed

*A note for readers of the old Sorcerie chapter.* This model replaces the old Arcana
rules entirely:

- **Arcana are slots in you, not items.** The Level-1-to-6 focus ladder, found-relic
  foci, and the example Arcana table are gone. Wands and staves are Wizard Drip now.
- **One gate, not two.** Sorcerie alone caps spell level. There is no vessel gate.
- **Bind/active/used/empty** becomes **ready/tapped/burned**. The binding hour becomes
  the one-hour swap; sleep still clears everything.
- **Unequip-and-it-snaps and disarm-the-mage are dead.** The hand is personal. Parting a
  mage from their books still cuts off redraws and swaps — the deck can be taken, the
  hand cannot.
- **Burning is new** — the flexible-casting valve, paid for in exiled future.

## Open Seams

*Small questions the new model raises, parked for a later pass:*

1. **Permanency** (Sorcerie 6) anchored a working in a permanently-active Arcana slot.
   New reading: the card stays *in play* forever and its Arcana slot is pinned — it cannot
   be redrawn, swapped, or converted back to physical while the working stands?
2. **Found magic drip.** Do relic items (a wand with its own working) grant a bonus card
   above hand size, a pinned card, or stay pure Referee territory?
3. **Potions** referenced Vitae brewing under the old Arcana section — unchanged in
   substance, but the text needs re-homing.
4. **Warding an ally** (Defend on their behalf) lives in the Defend rules and is
   untouched — confirm it needs no card language.
