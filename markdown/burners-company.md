# Burners Company

*Ok boys, check your kit, check your weapons, it's today. You get a pint and a chaser, no more. Let's go*

How a crew is hired, kept, bonded, and **Mustered**. Pay rates live on [[Burners Equipment#Hirelings]]. Heart 1+ unlocks Muster and Company slots in [[Burners Adventure Game]].

---

## Three Roles

| Role | What they are | Company seats | XP |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Hireling** | Day- or week-rate hand for this job | When a Boss Musters them | No |
| **Retainer** | On keep between jobs — standing hand, not yet bonded | When a Boss Musters them | No |
| **Henchman** | Sworn companion; trust earned in play | Yes — Mustered into Company seats | Bonus XP when they delved with the Boss |

Anyone with coin can hire. Only a **Boss (Heart 1+)** **Musters** — splitting the twelve into physical kit and **Company** seats, and seating today's crew.

---

## Ordinary Folk, Not Approach 0

Hands are not Burners. They have **no Approach levels**. Define them by **job Trait(s)** and kit — a fighting man with *spear* and *shield-wall* (two Sword-tagged Traits), a linkboy with *lantern-bearer*.

A Burner with **Approach 0** in Sword (levels elsewhere) still rolls `2d6` for Sword and has no Veteran tricks. That is a different sheet. Do not write "Sword 0" on a hireling.

In a fight they carry thin Fuel from kit, **no Veteran tricks**, and they are usually the first to die. Armor still soaks on the way in. Keep their sheet lean: Trait, Handling, kit, a margin note.

---

## Job Traits

A hireling, retainer, or henchman has **one Trait for their job**. They fill their item slots for that work. A highly skilled hand may hold a **second Trait** or a **Signature**.

**Packbearers** carry baggage and keep simple items handy: rope, waterskin, and the like.

**Fighting men** hold Sword-tagged Traits for preferred arms and kit — spear, arming sword, shield. They wear what the Boss outfits; steel and leather take their slots.

**Lantern bearers** (linkboys, torchbearers) hold *lantern-bearer* or *torchbearer*. They carry lantern or torches, flint and steel, oil.

**Shield bearers** hold *shield* / shield-bearer. They can raise a shield, but their chief work is resupplying fighters with fresh shields.

**Squires** keep weapons ready to hand off (*"Squire, my sword!"*). They may help a fighter don armor.

**War dogs** hold *bite* (and often a thick hide). A dog's "kit" is fangs and whatever collar or armor the fiction allows.

## Handling and Company Slots

Each hand needs **Handling** of **1 to 4**. That is how many **Company slots** a Boss must commit at Muster for them to do their job well.

- A docile, responsible torchbearer needs **1**.
- A violent or surly sellsword might need **4**.
- If a hand is not properly handled, they will not do the job well and may cause trouble.

The Referee sets Handling the first time the hand meets real pressure. Apply **+1 or +2** if the job is violence or unruliness. Later, when they have settled with the company, the Referee may lower it. Bonded henchmen who have stayed often sit lower than the day they signed on.

### Muster (Heart 1+)

Before a job — gathering the crew in town or at the safehouse — or on any **8-hour sleep**, the Boss **Musters**:

1. Declare the **physical / Company** split of the twelve.
2. Seat named hands into Company seats. Each person costs **Company slots equal to their Handling (1–4)**.
3. Lock the Muster for the run until the next Muster.

Going alone: Muster **0** Company, all twelve physical. Bonds wait at home.

Company slots hold **people**, not gear. Their kit rides on them; wages, keep, and new gear come from the Boss's purse. On Roll Initiative the Boss adds **one die per Company slot declared** (filled or empty). Those slots never also count as empty-inventory dice. See [[Burners Adventure Game]].

**Non-Bosses** may still hire for coin. Without Muster they do not split Company seats and do not gain Company-slot Initiative. Handling trouble is sharper without a Boss's Morale Check.

## Hirelings

Find them in the fiction: a tavern notice, a dockside crew, a debt called in. The offer is a Reaction roll (see [[Burners Referee Guide]]); Heart on the roll if you have it. A generous purse eases it; a bad name hardens it.

Pay the weekly rate on [[Burners Equipment#Hirelings]] and at least a **half-share** of the haul. Taking a hireling into a dungeon may raise the ask. **Food, lodging, and new gear are on you.** Paid hirelings earn **no XP**.

---

## Retainers

A **retainer** is on **keep** between jobs — standing hand, still coin, not yet bonded. Same job Trait, Handling, and kit rules. Field them at Muster like any other hand. Promote to henchman only when the bond is real (Referee's call): they stayed when it mattered, you kept your word, you pulled them out of the barrow.

---

## Henchmen

A **henchman** is a sworn companion: keep, a half-share of treasure, and the bond that lets them grow with you. Only a Boss may hold henchmen. Bonds are earned in play — not a Reaction roll on day one.

**On the delve.** Muster them into Company seats. They receive your Morale Check when the moment breaks. They want keep *and* at least a half-share.

**XP.** When the crew earns XP, figure the Boss's award first. Each henchman who **delved with you and survived** splits **half of your award** as **bonus XP** — not taken from you or the other PCs. One henchman present: 50% of what you gained. Two present: they split that half (25% each). Track their total on a margin line; it feeds the trainee path. Hirelings and retainers earn no XP.

**Replacing a henchman.** Death or departure ends the bond. Word of how you treat henchmen travels; abuse it and the next bond is harder to earn.

---

## Morale

When the company faces something that would break ordinary resolve (a fight turning, a horror from the crypt, a comrade cut down, wages long overdue), and a **Boss** leads the delve, the Referee calls a Morale Check for the affected hands:

> **Morale Check:** roll **`2d6 + your Heart`** against the moment's danger. A routine scare is low. A wraith clawing free of its tomb is high.
>
> - **Meet or beat → they hold.**
> - **Fall short → they break** — flee, freeze, drop the torch, or demand to leave. Play it by fiction.

Leading from the front and keeping your word ease the check; treating hands as arrow-fodder hardens it. Without a Boss, the Referee plays nerve by fiction or calls **`2d6` vs the danger** with no Heart bonus.

## Mercenaries (garrison)

Soldiers-for-hire for walls, camps, and armies — not barrow companions. Hire by the month; food and basic kit are in the rate; wages double in wartime.

| Troop | gp / month | Troop | gp / month |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Peasant levy | 1 | Archer | 5 |
| Light footman | 2 | Light horseman / longbowman | 10 |
| Heavy footman | 3 | Medium horseman / mounted archer | 15 |
| Crossbowman | 4 | Heavy horseman | 20 |

Their Morale is their own score (6 for a peasant levy up to 9 for mounted troops), checked on 2d6. A Boss on the field may steady them as on a delve.

---

## Trainee Adventurers

If a henchman survives hard jobs and their XP would reach the next character level on the table (they lag at half the Boss's pace), the table may promote them to a **trainee adventurer**. They shed Handling, begin earning Traits and Signatures, and climb toward a full Burner build. When your character dies, you may take a trainee as your new Burner.

---

## Professional Adventurers

The Referee may field a rare **professional adventurer**: an NPC built as a PC, with their own goals. Uncommon — nearly everyone with a real sheet is a player at the table.

## Example Hands

### Cob — lantern bearer (hireling)

- **Trait:** *lantern-bearer* (Craft-tagged).
- **Handling:** 1.
- **Kit:** hooded lantern, flask of oil, flint and steel, spare torch.
- Quiet, eyes on the floor, follows the Boss's heel. A pint settles him; a second one and he starts talking about home.

### Merrin — packbearer (hireling)

- **Trait:** *packbearer* (Craft-tagged).
- **Handling:** 1.
- **Kit:** rope, waterskins, spare rations, room on her back for the haul.
- Broad shoulders, few words. Will not go first into a dark room. Will not leave a pack behind.

### Joss — fighting man (hireling)

- **Traits:** *spear*, *leather* (Sword-tagged).
- **Handling:** 3 (+1 for a violent job).
- **Kit:** spear, leather armor (AC 1), short blade.
- Dockside muscle. Wants the fee in hand before the gate. Holds a line if the Boss is watching; looks for the door if not.

### Bren — shield bearer (retainer)

- **Trait:** *shield-bearer* (Sword-tagged). Signature: *fresh boards* — always seems to have one more shield in the cart.
- **Handling:** 2.
- **Kit:** two heaters, a spare buckler, leather.
- On keep. Resupplies the front when boards splinter. Ambition: leave the cart for a Mustered seat.

### Tam — squire (retainer)

- **Traits:** *squire*, *arming sword* (Sword-tagged).
- **Handling:** 2.
- **Kit:** master's spare blade sheathed, oil, strap kit; helps don mail.
- Young, proud of the Boss's harness. First into a squall of questions; last into a fight unless told.

### Rook — war dog (henchman)

- **Traits:** *bite*, *thick hide* (Sword-tagged).
- **Handling:** 2 (settled; was 3).
- **Kit:** the dog — collar, scars, a mean bark in the dark.
- Bonded after the fen job. Sleeps on the Boss's blanket. Does not share rations with strangers.

### Nell — spear (henchman)

- **Traits:** *spear*, *steady under fire* (Sword-tagged).
- **Handling:** 2 (settled from 4).
- **Kit:** spear, gambeson (AC 2), kettle helm.
- Starter bond for a green Boss — keep, half-share, and the one who checks the kit when the Muster is called. Surly once; not anymore.
