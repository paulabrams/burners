# Burners Company

*Ok boys, check your kit, check your weapons, it's today. You get a pint and a chaser, no more. Let's go*

How a crew is hired, kept, bonded, and **Mustered**. Pay rates live on [[Burners Equipment#Hirelings]]. Heart 1+ unlocks Muster and Company slots in [[Burners Adventure Game]].

---

## Two Roles

| Role | What they are | Dangerous places | Base Loyalty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Hireling** | Day- or week-rate hand | **No** — camp, wall, town only | **6** |
| **Retainer** | On keep; fights for pay | Yes | **7** |

**Loyal** is a **tag** applied to a hireling or a retainer after forging a loyal bond. A **loyal** hand gets a **substantial Loyalty bonus (+3)** and, when they hold, **acts loyally** — prioritizes their patron, Blocks for them, stays on their heel. Ordinary retainers put themselves first. Only a Boss's sworn company usually earns the tag in play; a camp hireling can be loyal too (they hold the wall for you, not because of a pay rate alone).

Anyone with coin can hire. Only a **Boss (Heart 1+)** **Musters** — splitting the twelve into physical kit and **Company** seats, and seating today's crew.

**XP.** Delving retainers take XP **as in OSE** — share the pot with the PCs, keep half of their award — whether Mustering or not. Levels on the Burner XP table (civilization), Sword or Craft only, never above the Boss's level. Hirelings do not delve.

---

## Company Hands (half power)

Hands are not Burners. They have a **track level** and place Approaches in **Sword or Craft only** — never Sorcerie, never Heart, never Muster. Hirelings usually stay at the level they were hired. Retainers climb on XP.

**Who plays them.**

- **Mustered** (in a Company seat): the **Boss's player** plays and rolls for that hand.
- **Otherwise** (hired but unseated, or hired by a non-Boss): **NPCs** run by the Referee.

Loyalty and Morale stay with the Referee either way.

**Everything that scales with level or Approach is halved, round down** (see *Manage* for the only round-up):

| Piece | Company rule |
| --- | --- |
| **Traits** | **2 per track level** (half of a Burner's 4) |
| **Signatures** | At most **⌊Approach / 2⌋** per Approach — earned in play, never bought |
| **HP** | Roll **1d6 per track level**, then **⌊total / 2⌋** (minimum **1**). On level-up: PC-style reroll (keep higher of new total or old+1), then half again. |
| **Fuel level dice / refill** | **⌊track level / 2⌋**, minimum **1** |
| **Veteran extras** | **⌊Sword / 2⌋** extras (Sword 1 → one attack) |
| **Approach rolls** | **`2d6 + ⌊Approach / 2⌋`** |
| **Shock and other Approach math** | Same half (Shock: **`2d6 + ⌊Sword / 2⌋`**) |

**Not halved:** wages, shares, Muster seats (**1 per hand**), Loyalty, kit on their person. Armor still soaks. Full Wound and Shock once HP is spent.

### Manage (Boss Action)

**Default:** half, always round **down**.

**Managed:** The Boss spends **their Action** that round to **manage** one **Mustered** hand. Until the next Roll Initiative, that hand uses **half round up** for Approach rolls, Shock, Veteran extras, and Fuel level-die counts in that window. HP on the sheet does not rewrite mid-fight.

- One Action → **one** hand for that round.
- Only a hand you are Mustering.
- Managing again next round costs another Action.

## Traits

Job names are Traits, tagged to Sword or Craft. A hand fills their item slots for that work; the Boss outfits upgrades from the purse.

**Packbearers** carry baggage and keep simple items handy: rope, waterskin, and the like.

**Fighting men** hold Sword-tagged Traits for preferred arms and kit — spear, arming sword, shield.

**Lantern bearers** (linkboys, torchbearers) hold *lantern-bearer* or *torchbearer*. Lantern or torches, flint and steel, oil.

**Shield bearers** hold *shield* / shield-bearer. Chief work: resupplying fighters with fresh boards.

**Squires** keep weapons ready (*"Squire, my sword!"*). They may help a fighter don armor.

**War dogs** hold *bite* (and often a thick hide).

## Company Seats

Each hand costs **one Company slot**. One person, one seat. Care, violence, and loyalty do not change seat cost — they modify **Loyalty** on Morale checks.

### Muster (Heart 1+)

Before a job — in town or at the safehouse — or on any **8-hour sleep**, the Boss **Musters**:

1. Declare the **physical / Company** split of the twelve.
2. Seat named hands — **one Company slot each**.
3. Lock the Muster for the run until the next Muster.

Going alone: Muster **0** Company, all twelve physical. Hands wait at home.

Company slots hold **people**, not gear. Kit rides on them; wages, keep, and new gear come from the Boss's purse. On Roll Initiative the Boss adds **one die per Company slot declared** (filled or empty). Those slots never also count as empty-inventory dice. See [[Burners Adventure Game]].

Company seats buy packing, Fuel, Initiative, Manage, and **Boss-player control** of that hand — not a different XP track.

**Non-Bosses** may still hire for coin. Without Muster they do not split Company seats and do not gain Company-slot Initiative.

## Hirelings

Find them in the fiction: a tavern notice, a dockside crew, a debt called in. Reaction roll (see [[Burners Referee Guide]]); Heart on the roll if you have it. Generous purse eases it; bad name hardens it.

**Track level** usually stays at hire (**1** — Sword or Craft, 2 Traits, half HP). **Loyalty 6.** They may gain the **loyal** tag (Loyalty 6+3 = **9**, act loyally) without becoming delvers.

Pay the weekly rate on [[Burners Equipment#Hirelings]]. **Will not enter dangerous places** — no barrow, no delve. Camp, wall, town, road. Food, lodging, and new gear are on you. **No XP.**

---

## Retainers

A **retainer** is on **keep** — standing hand, still coin. Half-power sheet; usually starts **level 1** and **climbs on XP**. They delve and fight; by default they **put themselves first**. **Loyalty 7.** Field them at Muster (**1 seat**). Signatures ≤ **⌊Approach / 2⌋** per Approach. Want keep and at least a half-share of treasure.

**XP:** Delving retainers take XP **as in OSE** (share the pot, keep half) — Mustering or not.

**Loyal.** When the bond is real (Referee's call), tag them **loyal**: +3 Loyalty, act loyally when they hold, keep and half-share as the bond asks. Same sheet and XP. The tag can fall if you abuse them — word travels.

**Starter (Boss).** A starting Boss begins with one **loyal retainer** at level 1: Sword 1 or Craft 1, 2 Traits, HP = ⌊1d6 / 2⌋ (min 1), Loyalty **10** (7+3), basic kit, keep and a share of the take.

## Morale (OSE-style)

The Referee calls **Morale** when nerve would break — unexpected peril in a fight, a horror rising, a comrade cut down, wages long overdue — and often **after an adventure** (will they stay on?).

> **Loyalty check:** roll **2d6**. If the roll is **≤ the hand's Loyalty** (with modifiers), they **hold**. If higher, they **break** — flee, freeze, drop the torch, demand to leave, or refuse further employment after the job. Play it by fiction.

**Base Loyalty:** hireling **6**, retainer **7**. **Loyal** tag: **+3**.

**Common modifiers** (Referee's call, as in OSE):

| Situation | Typical mod |
| --- | --- |
| Well kept (food, water, fair share, decent treatment) | +1 |
| Neglected (shorted pay/share, cold, mistreated) | −1 or −2 |
| Boss leading from the front / sharing the risk | + Boss's **Heart** |
| Boss down, fled, or absent | −2 |
| Overwhelming foe or sudden disaster | −1 or −2 |
| Generous treasure promised or just paid | +1 |

A **loyal** hand who holds **acts loyally** — they prioritize their patron over their own skin when the fiction allows. An ordinary retainer who holds still looks to their own hide first.

Without a Boss, the Referee may still check Loyalty against the base score with situational mods only.

## Mercenaries (garrison)

Soldiers-for-hire for walls, camps, and armies — not barrow companions. Hire by the month; food and basic kit in the rate; wages double in wartime.

| Troop | gp / month | Troop | gp / month |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Peasant levy | 1 | Archer | 5 |
| Light footman | 2 | Light horseman / longbowman | 10 |
| Heavy footman | 3 | Medium horseman / mounted archer | 15 |
| Crossbowman | 4 | Heavy horseman | 20 |

Their Morale is their own score (6 levy up to 9 mounted), checked on 2d6 as OSE. A Boss on the field may apply Heart and care mods as for company hands.

---

## Becoming a Burner

A company hand becomes a full **Burner** only when they become a **PC**. Until then they stay on the half-power track.

On that claim: PC **at their current track level**. Rebuild under full PC rules — 4 Traits per level, full HP, full Approach math, one Signature per Approach level, open Approaches.

---

## Professional Adventurers

The Referee may field a rare **professional adventurer**: an NPC built as a PC. Uncommon. Not a company upgrade path.

## Example Hands

All examples use half power (round down) unless a Boss **manages** them that round. Each Mustering hand costs **1** Company seat.

### Cob — lantern bearer (hireling)

- **Level 1, Craft 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **6**.
- **Traits (2):** *lantern-bearer*, *quiet* (Craft).
- **Kit:** hooded lantern, flask of oil, flint and steel, spare torch.
- Will not enter the barrow.

### Merrin — packbearer (hireling)

- **Level 1, Craft 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **6**.
- **Traits (2):** *packbearer*, *broad-backed* (Craft).
- **Kit:** rope, waterskins, spare rations, room for the haul.
- Camp and road only.

### Joss — fighting man (hireling)

- **Level 1, Sword 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **6** (−1 or −2 if shorted on the fee).
- **Traits (2):** *spear*, *leather* (Sword).
- **Kit:** spear, leather (AC 1), short blade.
- Walls and gates — not the dark below.

### Bren — shield bearer (retainer)

- **Level 1, Sword 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **7** (+1 well kept / −1 neglected).
- **Traits (2):** *shield-bearer*, *steady* (Sword).
- **Kit:** two heaters, spare buckler, leather.
- Puts self first; climbs on XP.

### Tam — squire (retainer)

- **Level 1, Sword 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **7**.
- **Traits (2):** *squire*, *arming sword* (Sword).
- **Kit:** master's spare blade, oil, strap kit.
- Proud of the harness; looks to his own skin when the line bucks.

### Rook — war dog (loyal retainer)

- **Level 1.** Sword 1. HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **10** (7+3 loyal).
- **Traits (2):** *bite*, *thick hide* (Sword).
- **Kit:** collar, scars, mean bark.
- Bonded after the fen job. When Morale holds, acts loyally. Sleeps on the Boss's blanket.

### Nell — spear (loyal retainer)

- **Level 1, Sword 1.** HP ⌊1d6/2⌋ (min 1). Loyalty **10** (7+3 loyal).
- **Traits (2):** *spear*, *steady under fire* (Sword).
- **Kit:** spear, gambeson (AC 2), kettle helm.
- Starter bond for a green Boss. Keep, half-share. When she holds, she prioritizes the Boss.
