# Burners — Magic Examples

*Every 1st-level working, ruled at the table — the World engine and the Combat engine, side by side.*

A spell does what its entry says. Casting is never a roll to see whether the magic works;
the roll, when there is one, prices what the working costs you or contests it with a
target. The full procedure is on [[Burners Sorcerie]]; the catalog is [[Burners Spells]].

- **World engine.** Say what you do and the working lands. If the moment is risky, the
  Referee names a Cost — quoted against the outcome you want and the pressure of the
  scene, not the spell's level — and you roll **2d6 + Sorcerie** (Craft for Canting) to
  pay less. Small ask in a quiet scene, small Cost. No price on the table, no roll.
- **Combat engine.** Casting is one Action: burn at least SL Fuel dice (SL = the spell's
  level). Only a working aimed at an unwilling target uses the **cast total** — the
  target Defends, and overrun lands the harm or the effect. A utility or sensory working
  simply happens; extra Fuel past SL buys nothing there.
- **What the magic causes is a new question.** An illusory sound may call for a Morale
  check; a fog changes position and cover; a friendly face still will not open the vault.
  Those follow their own rules, and neither the cast total nor the caster's dice modify
  them unless a spell says so.

In the entries below, **Sorcerie** counts (a number of creatures, turns, feet) read as
the caster's Sorcerie level — Craft for Canting — exactly as in the catalog.

## Sorcerie

#### Read Magic

- **World.** A captured grimoire in a strange hand: cast *Read Magic* and the script
  opens — you know what each working is, and the book joins your readable sources. The
  plain reading is free; probing deeper for Facts is a Step-2 risk (see *Reading Magic*
  on [[Burners Sorcerie]]).

#### Detect Magic

- **World.** Sweeping the reliquary before the abbot returns: for Sorcerie turns,
  anything magical in sight glows to your eye, and you may ask its rough school and
  strength up to Sorcerie times. The sweep itself is free; if lingering is the danger,
  the Referee quotes the Cost against Heat, not against the spell.

#### Ward Pact

- **Combat.** You readied the card with **fire** named. Cast it as the salamander
  closes: for Sorcerie turns its flame bites you for −Sorcerie Damage. The choice that
  mattered was made at ready time — a ward named for blades is no help here, and the
  magic pact (the old *Parma Magica*) is its own naming.

#### Wizard Light

- **World.** Light on a shield boss before the stairs: it burns torch-bright, radius
  30', for Sorcerie hours. No roll — light is the whole working. Quenching the
  guardroom's lanterns from the doorway is the same spell with a price; the Referee
  quotes what the sudden dark may cost.
- **Combat.** Cast on a foe's eyes it is hostile: one Action, at least 1 Fuel for the
  cast total, and the target Defends or is blind for 1 round.

#### Read Languages

- **World.** The smuggler's ledger is in cipher: for Sorcerie turns you read it plain —
  maps, codes, dead scripts, anything mundane. If the watchman's round is coming, time
  is the price the Referee quotes.

#### Cantrip

- **World.** Chill the wine, colour the hearth-flame, snap a spark into the tinder.
  Trivial and safe, so it simply happens. A cantrip never deals damage, moves real
  weight, or fools a careful eye — so it never asks for a roll.

## Vitae

#### Animal Bond

- **World.** Touch the miller's hound and it regards you as a trusted companion for
  Sorcerie months. The working is easy; explaining to the miller why his dog follows
  you out of town is the fiction, and the Referee prices that, not the cast.

#### Animal Friendship

- **World.** The kennel dogs obey you as well as a trained dog obeys its master, for
  one day: *down*, *quiet*, *stay*. Getting near enough to cast under the handler's eye
  is where the risk lives.
- **Combat.** Cast on beasts already at your throat it is hostile control: each Defends
  against your cast total, and on overrun the pack stands down for the day.

#### Beast Speech

- **World.** Ask the ravens what crossed the pass at dawn. Speech is all you get — they
  answer as their nature moves them, and persuading them to act is Heart, as with
  anyone.

#### Healing Touch

- **World.** In camp after the ambush, a willing ally above 0 HP takes **1d6** back, or
  one open limb wound closes. No Cost when nothing presses.
- **Combat.** One Action, at least 1 Fuel: touch an ally above 0 HP and restore 1d6. At
  0 HP or less the card does nothing — that work is *Stanch*.

#### Joy Song

- **World.** The crew stands shaken at the barrow mouth: Sorcerie listeners shake off
  the dread, and each with at least 1 HP recovers 1. It lasts the scene.
- **Combat.** Sung with blades out it is still one Action and 1 Fuel; an ally gripped by
  fear or despair shakes it off as the entry says. Nothing opposes it, so extra Fuel
  buys nothing.

#### Light Shield

- **Combat.** Cast on Brand before the crypt door opens: blows by undead or unbound
  spirits against him suffer −1 Damage for Sorcerie turns, and a weak spirit's charm or
  possession fails against him outright. A willing ally is not a contest — no Defend, no
  cast total to weigh. #needs-playtest

#### Stanch

- **World.** The caravan guard is bleeding out on the road, at 0 HP or less: your touch
  restores **1d6 HP** and stabilizes him enough to count as help. Maimings stay closed
  to it.
- **Combat.** Sefa is down past 0: one Action, 1 Fuel, and she is stable and breathing.
  *Healing Touch* cannot do this work; this card can do no other.

## Elementale

#### Crystal Growth

- **World.** Overnight, Sorcerie fist-sized crystals grow from the cell wall — hard as
  stone, worth little at market, and just the thing to jam under a door or brace a bar.
  Time is the only price unless somebody is watching.

#### Dragon's Breath

- **World.** Burning the web-choked corridor clear: the flame does what flame does. The
  Referee prices the smoke and the light — a Cost against Heat — not the burning
  itself.
- **Combat.** A 60' cone: every creature caught Defends separately against the same
  cast total, and overrun goes to HP (− AC if physical; monsters → Hits).

#### Fog Cloud

- **World.** Fog rolls out Sorcerie × 10' around you at the dock's edge and the crew
  walks out of sight. It fades in a turn; what the harbor watch makes of a sudden fog
  is the Referee's to price, if anything.
- **Combat.** No target, no Defend — the bank simply spreads. What it does to archers,
  pursuit, and cover is fiction the Referee rules on.

#### Heat Metal

- **World.** The strongbox's iron hasp glows until the wax seals run and the lock's
  temper goes — quiet work in an empty counting-house, priced only if discovery is on
  the table.
- **Combat.** One foe's sword glows hot: the holder takes **1d6** to HP (− AC if physical; monsters → Hits)
  each round for Sorcerie turns, unless they drop it. As written the drop is the
  defense — the spell offers no Defend roll.

#### Icy Touch

- **World.** Ice spreads across the millpond's sluice gate and seals it shut until thaw
  or hammer.
- **Combat.** Ice the flagstones between you and the charge: a floor cannot Defend.
  What slick footing does — a rush halted, a foe in the dirt — is the Referee's ruling
  on the fiction.

#### Web Weave

- **World.** Strung across the stream at dusk, the web nets breakfast by morning — a
  small ask at no pressure, **Cost 2–4**, roll **2d6 + Sorcerie** to pay less. The same
  web sealing a door mid-pursuit is the same spell at a far steeper quote.
- **Combat.** Webbing a doorway is placement: 1 Fuel, no Defend, and it holds until
  burned. Shooting the web at a creature is hostile — it Defends against your cast
  total, and overrun means stuck.

## Thaumaturgy

#### Adhere

- **World.** Paint the ledge sticky and the crew climbs the overhang boots-to-stone, no
  rope. Washing is the only undoing.
- **Combat.** Sticking a foe's boots to the flagstones is hostile: Defend against the
  cast total; overrun holds them fast until washed.

#### Command

- **World.** *"Sleep,"* to the gate guard. A hostile working with no fight underway
  still rolls initiative — the whole alley rolls with what it holds — and the guard
  Defends against your cast total. One word, and nothing that harms him.
- **Combat.** *"Flee."* *"Drop."* *"Kneel."* One Action, at least 1 Fuel, Defend and
  overrun as any control.

#### Thaumaturgic Hand

- **World.** The key hangs on a nail across the portcullis: the spectral hand fetches
  it. Light objects only, for Sorcerie turns — it cannot attack or exert real force, in
  or out of a fight.

#### Filch

- **World.** The magistrate's seal sits on his desk; it is in your hand from across the
  room. Being caught holding it is the Cost the Referee prices.
- **Combat.** Filching the torch from an orc's grip is contested: it Defends against
  your cast total, and on overrun the light is yours.

#### Force Push

- **World.** The toppled column blocking the door slides away — an object of any size,
  pushed with the strength of Sorcerie men for one round. Noise and time are what the
  Referee prices.
- **Combat.** Shove the loaded cart into the shield line: the cart is the object, and
  what the impact does to the men behind it — a blow of situational dice, bodies in the
  dirt — is the Referee's ruling.

#### Leap

- **World.** The chasm is 30' and your Sorcerie is 3: you jump it. No roll — the spell
  is the bridge. A leap under crossbow fire prices the exposure, not the distance.
- **Combat.** Cast, and the movement comes with your Action: over the shield line and
  down behind the sergeant. The leap itself contests nothing.

## Illusione

#### Arcane Eye

- **World.** The eye drifts down the stairwell and around two corners for Sorcerie
  turns; you count the sentries through it as clearly as with your own eyes. Scouting
  is the working — what you do with the count is the plan.

#### Auditory Illusion

- **World.** A watchman's whistle, two streets over, and the foot patrol turns toward
  it. Whether they bite is the Referee's read of the patrol — perhaps a Reaction roll.
  Your dice made the sound; they do not make the decision.
- **Combat.** One Action, 1 Fuel, and a nest-mother's shriek fills the gallery. The
  giant spiders' answer is a **Morale check** — 2d6 against their score, one roll for
  the pack — if the Referee judges the sound would frighten them at all. Six Fuel buy
  no louder a lie than one.

#### Bewitch

- **World.** The clerk believes you are a close friend — a disposition, not a command
  and not a Muster slot. He waves you past the anteroom; he will not open the
  strongroom, betray his master, or forget his duty, and asks against his interest run
  through Influence as with any friend. Proven otherwise — your knife at his ledger —
  and the friendship ends.
- **Combat.** Direct control is hostile even in a parlor: initiative rolls, you spend
  at least 1 Fuel for the cast total, the target Defends (an adjacent Sorcerer may
  Ward), and only overrun makes the friendship. The sharper's failed charm in
  [[Burners Examples of Play]] runs the whole procedure.

#### Blur

- **Combat.** Cast on yourself as the arrows start: while you move, every attack
  against you suffers −Sorcerie Damage for Sorcerie turns. Nothing opposes it — the
  working shaves blows on the way in; your Fuel still Defends them.

#### Visual Illusion

- **World.** A stack of crates that was never there hides the bolt-hole. Silent,
  motionless, no smell — anyone who touches or studies it sees through, so the Referee
  prices how closely the room looks, not the image.
- **Combat.** An illusory spearman in the doorway strikes nobody and blocks nothing.
  Whether the gnolls press past him is Morale or the Referee's read — the same routing
  as the shriek and the spiders.

#### Detect Magic (Illusione)

- **World.** The keener working: for a day, anything magical in sight glows and names
  its properties when asked, up to Sorcerie requests. Reading the hoard back at camp
  costs nothing but the card.

## Umbrakala

#### Befuddle

- **World.** The steward will remember nothing of the next Sorcerie turns — but
  unmaking a mind is hostile even in his own study: initiative rolls, and he Defends
  against your cast total before the working takes him.

#### Cloak of Shadows

- **Combat.** In torchlight and gloom you fight at +Sorcerie AC for Sorcerie turns. It
  is soak, not stealth: blows still land and Fuel still Defends; the shadow just
  thickens the skin.

#### Darksight

- **World.** Douse the torches and walk the tunnels seeing plain, for Sorcerie hours.
  The rest of the crew still needs light — that tension is fiction, not a roll.

#### Deep Shadow

- **World.** A sphere of total darkness swallows the courtyard and the crew crosses
  under the walls. What the sentries do about a hole in the night is the Referee's
  call — perhaps nothing, perhaps the alarm.
- **Combat.** Dropped over the archers' platform it targets nobody and allows no
  Defend. Loosing blind into the black is the fiction the Referee rules on.

#### Gate Sense

- **World.** For Sorcerie turns you feel the thin places within Sorcerie × 100' — the
  sealed Kalla gate behind the crypt wall hums against your teeth. Finding is the
  spell; opening is another matter entirely.

#### Hatred

- **Combat.** Cast on the two bandit spearmen: hostile control, so each Defends against
  the same cast total, and those overrun turn on each other for a turn or until one
  dies. Cast where no fight is yet underway, it starts one — roll initiative first.

## Necromantia

#### Animate Skeleton

- **World.** Sorcerie skeletons claw up from the barrow floor and dig, haul, and carry
  until told otherwise. Unarmed dead make poor company at a town gate; the Referee
  prices being seen.
- **Combat.** They serve as the Referee runs them: unarmed dead of the meanest sort —
  bodies to fill a doorway, not blades to win the fight.

#### Black Sacrament

- **World.** At dusk you open a vein — 1 HP — and bless your Sorcerie undead servants
  for the day: +1 Damage on every blow they land tonight. The price is paid at the
  casting; the fight just collects.

#### Blood Curse

- **World.** Mark the fleeing cultist and for Sorcerie days you know his direction and
  distance. Marking an unwilling man is hostile — he Defends against the cast total. A
  willing ally takes the mark freely, for the finding.

#### Blood Track

- **World.** The wounded wyvern bled as it flew: follow the trail up to Sorcerie miles
  while it is fresh. Following is free; what waits at the end is its own scene.

#### Marble Madness

- **World.** Your pockets refill with marbles every round for Sorcerie rounds — spill
  them down the stair behind you and let the pursuit find its footing. What marbles do
  to a running man is the Referee's ruling.
- **Combat.** Strewn across the flagstones they are ground, not a blow: no Defend, but
  a charging foe may end **in the dirt** if the Referee rules the scramble.

#### Wizard Mark

- **World.** Paint the junctions as you go: ulfire marks only you can see, at any
  distance, even through walls. The maze unknots for free; the card is the cost.

## Canting

*Canting reads **Craft** wherever a count says Sorcerie, and rolls **2d6 + Craft** in
the world. See *Canting: Kicks and Jarks* on [[Burners Sorcerie]].*

#### Budge

- **World.** The warehouse's back door and its padlock click open — Craft locks at
  once, and no pick marks left behind. The Referee prices the noise of the door and the
  timing of the round, not the mechanism.

#### Cony-Catch

- **World.** One look at the fence and you hold a sentence of truth — his vice or his
  fear. The spell hands you the wedge, not the win: working it is a Heart squeeze like
  any other.

#### Flash Paper

- **World.** The job's terms pass from hand to hand as blank paper; only
  Canting-literate eyes read them, and after Craft readings the sheet burns itself. A
  dead drop that cleans up after — no roll unless the passing is the risky part.

#### Juke

- **Combat.** Dirty fighting in a card: your next Defend this round gains +Craft. Spend
  it as the bravo's knife comes in — the bonus rides a Defend you were paying for
  anyway.

#### Mark the Gull

- **World.** For Craft hours you pick out the most gullible or greedy mark in any
  crowd, and Heart contests against you fail. Choosing the mark is the spell; the con
  is still played at the table.

#### Mort's Mask

- **World.** Craft humanoids wear new faces past the gate — until one of them speaks.
  The silence is the discipline; the Referee prices the walk through the checkpoint,
  not the working.
